Background

The Project

began in August 2021, funded for two years by Berlin University Alliance.

The Idea

Providing an overview over Digital Humanities projects working with SWANA Non-Latin Scripts (e.g. Arabic, Hebrew) in Germany.

Open Scholarship

The project should be completely OpenSource, OpenAccess and commit to FAIR principles.

Interoperability

We use a github repository with a file-based database for better sustainability and easier deployment. At the moment, we use .json as the export and working format, other formats (.xml, .yaml) are planned.

Collaboration

The project aims to provide a low-level collaboration workflow for researchers to add their own projects to the database
("By the community for the community").
