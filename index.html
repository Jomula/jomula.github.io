Arabic Vernacular Poetry

Challenges in Working with Arabic Script, Speech and (Re)Presentation

Research Data

Arabic vernacular poetry, created by nomadic and semi-nomadic internees during and after their internment in Italian concentration camps in Libia Coloniale in the late 1920s and early 1930s.

Research Project

Read the poems - that were yet only addressed as reports on what happened in the concentration camps - as subjective voices of internees.

Address how they dealt with their being in a traumatizing environment in the context of Arabic poetry traditions.

Sources and Problems

  • Original mode unknown, presumably oral in most cases.
  • Collected as part of a national narrative-building process in the Ǧamāhirīya.
  • Transcribed in non-vocalized Arabic Script.
  • Only parts of the material published but bad editing quality.
  • No digital copies or openAccess archives.
Arabic Script ...
  • ... is an impure Abjad, with long vowel signs and only optional vowel diacritics.
  • ... is written from right to left.
  • ... consists of letters with mostly contextual letterforms.
  • ... is a highly inflected language.
  • ... can be transcribed in Latin Script, but transcription systems differ depending on the language.

I used TEI because ...

  • ... it is a well documented and extensive standard.
  • ... is highly interoperable.
  • ... XML can be simply stored as files or ingested in an XML-database without further transformation or querying.
  • ... it offers the option to embed CMIF to the header of some of the poems that were sent as letters.
  • ... well, I like it.

... BUT ...

  • ... RTL-Script is pain in an LTR-environment.
  • ... No dedicated support for (oral or semi-oral) Arabic in general.
  • ... Thus, no dedicated support for the specifics of Arabic poetry.
  • ... Thus, no dedicated support for the specifics of my sources whatsoever.

How to deal with it?

Three TEI-XML files for each poem:

  • One for the Arabic original.
  • One for the translation (used for encoding rhetorics).
  • One for the transcription (used for language-specific encoding). [skipped]

Issues

Technical Issues
  • No proper RTL/LTR-support in openSource-IDEs.
  • RTL in an XML-LTR environment.

⇒ Complex encoding renders text almost illegible for humans and makes it extremely prone to encoding-errors.

Language-related Issues
  • Pronounciation and thus information on oral performativity is not represented in Arabic Script (Abjad).
  • Loss of information due to the poems long transmission.
And what about epistemic violence?
Despite me (white, European, no native speaker of Arabic) working on a dataset of bad quality in a field that is mostly unknown yet highly disputed, challenging the way, in which this poetry was addressed in Libyan nationalist narratives (and still is by this handful of scholars), using Latin-Script-based IDEs and markup languages and in general Latin-Script-based infrastructure that fails to address the specifics of Arabic?
  • Transcription as a workaround is problematic because of its «scriptual coloniality» and its ambiguity.
  • Loss of information (availability of sources, transmission, abjad, transcription, translation, limited language skills) may lead to false presumptions, interpretations and thus false ideas of representability.
  • No proper support in IDEs requires LS-based workarounds or a strong will to suffer.
  • Arabic Script remains marginalized in digital humanities, even more so dialectal representations.
«Why not use solutions like OpenITI mARkdown?»

Despite it being developed mainly for «tagging structural, morphological and semantic elements in premodern and early modern Islamicate texts» and not modern vernacular poetry?

  • I find it much less intuitive and readable than XML.
  • Lack of Stack-related community.
  • It «is not meant to be a comprehensive standard».
  • It is yet another Latin-Script-based workaround solution.
«Why not use Gedit with its better RTL-support?»

It is an OperatingSystem- and WindowManager-specific text editor with coding support, not a Fullstack-IDE. It doesn't really work on MacOS, at least not reliably. If we require data to be interoperable, so should be the IDE in terms of OS.

Where to go from here?

  • Are there better pragmatic approaches for a case like mine?
  • How to improve NLS-support in XML (or other markup languages)? Would a TEI-SIG for (SWANA-)NLS be an option to improve the situation?
  • How to deal with stack- and infrastructure-related issues of epistemic violence?
  • How to deal with the IDE situation?
